TRADE FINANCE

Trade finance is the oldest form of financing and is vital to the global economy. It facilitates business to business trade in goods and services between exporters and importers.

Global banks are best in class in providing trade finance due to the depth and the breath of their global footprint. Trafaltin believes that bank-originated trade finance credit claims could represent:

A source of alpha with very low correlation to public fixed income markets;
Returns determined by a floating rate benchmark (i.e. Libor) plus a credit margin;
Short duration due to very short tenors (30 - 360 days);
Short duration due to floating rate pricing.

The low credit-loss characteristics of Trade Finance are illustrated by the ICC Trade Register. This database contains historic Trade Finance credit performance data from 25 participating banks, more than 20 million transactions and more than 11 USD trillion exposure. (ICC Trade Register 2017)
